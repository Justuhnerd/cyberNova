Know Your Enemy

The Cyber Threat Landscape

Understanding threats targeting Southern African organisations is the first line of defence.

Full threat education centre arrow_forward

bug_report

Malware

Malicious software including viruses, trojans, and spyware. A leading threat against African SMEs with limited endpoint protection.

lock

Ransomware

Encrypts organisational data and demands cryptocurrency payment. RaaS operations target Southern African logistics, health, and finance sectors.

phishing

Phishing

Deceptive emails, smishing, or calls impersonating trusted entities. Load-shedding and banking themes are prevalent in South Africa.

storm

DDoS Attacks

Floods infrastructure with traffic to render services unavailable. African financial institutions and government portals face increasing DDoS campaigns.

radar

APTs

Nation-state groups maintaining long-term covert network presence. APTs targeting African government ministries have risen sharply since 2022.

emergency

Zero-Day Exploits

Attacks exploiting unknown vulnerabilities before patches exist. Critical for organisations running legacy ERP and banking systems common in the SADC region.

router

IoT Vulnerabilities

Smart meters and IP cameras with weak credentials create entry points. Africa's rapid IoT rollout outpaces security hardening.

psychology

Social Engineering

Manipulating staff through impersonation and urgency. BEC scams targeting CFOs are among the highest-cost attacks in African finance.

manage_accounts

Insider Threats

Malicious or negligent employees with legitimate access. High-privilege accounts in government and banking present the greatest insider risk exposure.

output

Data Exfiltration

Unauthorised transfer of customer records or financial data. POPIA mandates breach notification within 72 hours.
